# iPRACS — Intelligence in Processes, Advanced Catalysts and Solvents

Welcome to the GitHub organization of the **iPRACS research group** at the University of Antwerp.

iPRACS aims to transform how innovations in circular and sustainable chemical processes are developed by integrating **early-stage sustainability assessment** with **experimental and in silico research**. This combined approach enables process design optimization at low technology readiness levels, improving both sustainability and feasibility while increasing research efficiency.

Our work bridges chemistry and engineering through a holistic methodology that couples technological process research with sustainability modeling, supported by close intra- and extra-mural collaborations. The group focuses on **advanced catalysts and solvents** for organic materials synthesis and recovery, complemented by state-of-the-art **life cycle assessment (LCA)** and **techno-economic analysis (TEA)** to guide sustainable process design.

## Research areas

Our research spans multiple domains, including:

- Sustainability assessment and decision modeling  
- Circular polymeric materials and chemical recycling  
- Catalyst heterogenization (e.g., MOFs)  
- Tailored monomers from renewable resources  
- Designer solvents such as NADES  

These topics collectively support the development of circular and sustainable chemical technologies.

## Purpose of this GitHub organization

This organization hosts:

- Research code and computational tools  
- Data analysis workflows  
- Modeling and simulation software  
- Reproducible research resources  
- Collaboration templates and documentation  

Our repositories support open science, reproducibility, and collaboration within the sustainable chemistry and process engineering community.
